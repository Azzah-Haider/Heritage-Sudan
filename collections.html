Mojo Gallery

Mojo Gallery serves as a vibrant hub for Sudanese art enthusiasts. Established in 2010, it aims to showcase and sell works that reflect Sudan's rich cultural tapestry.

Founded to elevate Sudanese art, the gallery features exhibitions that highlight various visual styles influenced by both African and Arab cultures. The architecture is simple yet functional, enhancing the presentation of artworks without distraction.

  • Established: 2010
  • Mission: Showcase and sell Sudanese art
  • Focus: Contemporary Sudanese art
  • Architecture: Simple, functional design
  • Cultural Influence: African and Arab visual styles
  • Role: Cultural hub for art enthusiasts
Sudan Art Diwan

Sudan Art Diwan offers an immersive experience into the dynamic world of Sudanese art, featuring monoprints, etchings, and miniatures. This gallery showcases the vitality of Sudanese aesthetics on an international level.

Founded with the aim to promote local artists, Sudan Art Diwan serves as a cultural hub in Khartoum. It highlights diverse artistic expressions and provides a space for dialogue between creators and the community. The architectural design reflects traditional Sudanese elements adorned with modern touches.

  • Art Forms: Monoprints, etchings, miniatures
  • Mission: Promote local Sudanese artists
  • Role: Cultural hub in Khartoum
  • Architecture: Traditional Sudanese elements with modern touches
  • Focus: Diverse artistic expressions
  • Community Role: Space for dialogue between artists and community
  • International Reach: Showcases Sudanese aesthetics globally
Dara Art Gallery

Dara Art Gallery is a Sudanese art gallery founded in late 1999 by the artists Rashid Diab and Mercedes Carmona at their house in Khartoum. They envisioned the creation of a space where artists from Sudan could exhibit their work in a professional manner.

Since its opening it has been host to many of the great Sudanese artists including Ibrahim Al-Salahi, Yassir Abu-El-Haram, Hussein Gamaan, Ahmad Mohamad Shibrain and many more. Of course over the years Rashid Diab and Mercedes Carmona have also had their work displayed in the gallery.

In 2005 Rashid Diab opened the Rashid Diab Arts Centre. That space was then designated to furthering the opportunities for Sudanese artists that Dara Art Gallery once offered. Since 2005 the gallery has been exclusively Rashid Diab's private gallery and has been exhibiting his work from the 80s all the way till the present day.

As of 2020 the gallery has come under new management, and the works of more artists have been added. Furthermore, during the recent pandemic there has been a re-cataloguing, archiving and restoring of Rashid Diab's work. Unfortunately, as of 2023 the war in Sudan has seen the capital Khartoum completely pillaged and the gallery has embarked on a nomadic journey. We are now limited to international art fairs and other international curatorial projects.

  • Founded: Late 1999
  • Founders: Rashid Diab and Mercedes Carmona
  • Original Location: Founders' house in Khartoum
  • Featured Artists: Ibrahim Al-Salahi, Yassir Abu-El-Haram, Hussein Gamaan, Ahmad Mohamad Shibrain
  • 2005: Rashid Diab opens Rashid Diab Arts Centre
  • Post-2005: Became Rashid Diab's private gallery
  • 2020: New management, more artists added
  • Current Status: Nomadic due to war in Sudan, participating in international art fairs
  • Special Projects: Re-cataloguing and restoration of Rashid Diab's work
Remenar Gallery

Remenar Gallery is contemporary and visual art gallery in Sudan where you can view and shop for original paintings as well as art pieces of heritage, various handicrafts, and antiques and accessories.

  • Type: Contemporary and visual art gallery
  • Products: Original paintings, heritage art pieces, handicrafts, antiques, accessories
  • Hours: Daily 8:30 AM – 4:30 PM
  • Closed: Fridays
  • Services: Viewing and shopping for art
  • Collection: Mix of contemporary art and traditional heritage pieces
The Downtown Gallery

Also known as "The Hidden Gem Of Alsoog Alarabi" and Sudan's underground art gallery, The Downtown Gallery is Sol Creatives and Downtown Hotel & Business Center's joint project to transform the concept of art curation in Sudan. With a wall that fits up to 18 pieces of artwork and an art store attached, this small space is designed to make the visitor live the experience.

  • Also Known As: "The Hidden Gem Of Alsoog Alarabi", Sudan's underground art gallery
  • Partnership: Sol Creatives and Downtown Hotel & Business Center
  • Mission: Transform art curation concept in Sudan
  • Location: Khartoum 2
  • Hours: 9 AM – 7 PM
  • Capacity: Wall fits up to 18 artwork pieces
  • Features: Art store attached, immersive experience
  • Concept: Small space designed for visitor experience
