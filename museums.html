National Museum of Sudan

The National Museum of Sudan can be regarded as the main guardian of Sudan's historical heritage, as it documents, preserves, and displays all ancient cultural materials collected in Sudan for the benefit of visitors. Established in 1971 at an attractive site on Nile Street overlooking the Blue Nile before its confluence with the White Nile.

The museum consists of two main sections. The first is an outdoor garden with lush green spaces intersected by a water channel symbolizing the Nile. Here, monuments and temples from the Napatan and Christian periods were reconstructed after being rescued from flooding caused by the construction of the Aswan Dam in the 1960s.

The second section is a two-storey building that houses a vast collection of rare artifacts covering all stages and eras of Sudanese civilizations, from the early Paleolithic period through the Napatan, Meroitic, Christian, and Islamic periods. Special attention is given to the collection of rare artifacts belonging to the Kerma civilization (2500–1800 BC).

  • Location: Nile Street, Khartoum
  • Hours: 8:30 AM – 6:30 PM (Closed on Mondays)
  • Established: 1971
  • Special Features: Outdoor garden with reconstructed temples, two-storey exhibition building
Sudan Ethnographic Museum

Established in 1956, the museum is located at the intersection of University Street and Al-Mak Nimir Street. It houses an impressive collection of the material culture of various Sudanese tribes and peoples. This rich heritage of agricultural and nomadic tools, household utensils, ceremonial instruments, and facilities is displayed in an artistic and educational manner.

  • Location: Intersection of University Street and Al-Mak Nimir Street, Khartoum
  • Hours: 8:30 AM – 5:00 PM (Closed on Mondays)
  • Established: 1956
  • Collection: Material culture of Sudanese tribes including tools, utensils, and ceremonial items
Natural History Museum

This museum is located on University Street, opposite the main building of the University of Khartoum. It was established during the second decade of this century and later affiliated with the Faculty of Science. The museum displays interesting and unique species of animals and birds found in Sudan, including a collection of preserved reptiles. It also exhibits some live snakes as well as certain birds and animals.

  • Location: University Street, opposite University of Khartoum
  • Hours: 8:30 AM – 6:00 PM (Closed on Mondays)
  • Established: Early 20th century
  • Collection: Animals, birds, reptiles of Sudan (including live specimens)
  • Affiliation: Faculty of Science, University of Khartoum
Khalifa House Museum

This was the residence of Khalifa Abdallah ibn Muhammad al-Ta'aishi, the successor of the Mahdi. The ground floor of the house was built in 1888 and the upper floor in 1891. It became a museum in 1928. The building itself has great historical significance, reflecting the architectural style of that period. The extensive collection of artifacts displayed in the two-storey museum covers almost the entire Mahdist period, in addition to the Khalifa's personal belongings such as utensils, beds, swords, spears, and various other items of daily use.

  • Location: Omdurman, Khartoum State
  • Hours: 8:30 AM – 6:30 PM (Closed on Mondays)
  • Built: 1888 (ground floor), 1891 (upper floor)
  • Museum Since: 1928
  • Collection: Mahdist period artifacts, Khalifa's personal belongings
  • Architecture: Historic Mahdist-era residential building
Republican Palace Museum

The Republican Palace Museum is one of the important museums in Khartoum. It houses historical collections belonging to the Republican Palace, the seat of government in Sudan, covering successive historical periods of modern Sudanese history. The museum also includes a variety of other archaeological and heritage artifacts.

It is the first museum of its kind to focus on the possessions of Sudan's rulers from the period 1884 to 2007. The museum was officially inaugurated on December 31, 1999.

  • Location: Republican Palace Complex, Khartoum
  • Inaugurated: December 31, 1999
  • Collection Focus: Possessions of Sudan's rulers (1884-2007)
  • Significance: First museum focusing on presidential collections
  • Content: Historical collections from Republican Palace, archaeological artifacts
Sheikan Museum

This museum is located in the city of El Obeid, North Kordofan State. The name Sheikan is derived from a site near El Obeid, where the Mahdist forces defeated a large British force in the Battle of Sheikan in 1883.

The museum was established in 1965 and contains a large number of historical and ethnographic items that reflect the different stages of Kordofan's history. In addition, it displays artifacts ranging from the Early Stone Age (Paleolithic) to the Islamic Funj period (1505–1820). The museum also exhibits some artifacts and interesting items from the Battle of Sheikan.

  • Location: El Obeid, North Kordofan State
  • Hours: 8:30 AM daily (Closed on Mondays)
  • Established: 1965
  • Named After: Battle of Sheikan (1883)
  • Collection: Kordofan history artifacts from Paleolithic to Funj period, Battle of Sheikan items
  • Focus: Historical and ethnographic items of Kordofan region
Sultan Ali Dinar Museum

This museum is located in the city of El Fasher, North Darfur State, western Sudan. It contains a remarkable collection of archaeological items tracing the history of the region since ancient times, with special attention given to the house and possessions of Sultan Ali Dinar, the last sultan of Darfur.

  • Location: El Fasher, North Darfur State
  • Focus: History of Darfur region and Sultan Ali Dinar
  • Collection: Archaeological items from ancient times, personal possessions of Sultan Ali Dinar
  • Significance: Preserves the heritage of the Darfur Sultanate
  • Cultural Value: Important for understanding Western Sudan history
War Museum

The museum serves as a military archive, preserving the belongings of the Sudanese Armed Forces and documenting their long history rooted in the heroism of ancestors. It aims to strengthen relations between the armed forces and citizens, reminding them of the national role of protecting the homeland.

  • Purpose: Military archive and historical documentation
  • Collection: Belongings of Sudanese Armed Forces, military artifacts
  • Mission: Strengthen relations between armed forces and citizens
  • Educational Value: Documents military history and national protection
  • Cultural Role: Preserves military heritage and heroism
Sudanese Railways Museum

The museum was established by the Sudanese Railways Corporation in the year 2000. It was created in line with railway museums around the world, with the aim of preserving and documenting the long-standing heritage of the railways. The museum is located in the city of Atbara, in the Al-Sudanah district.

The museum contains many historically valuable collections, including wooden and iron sleepers, steam and diesel locomotives manufactured in 1901, railway carriages from 1901, 1904, and 1912, an English-made "Garrick" automobile from 1949, and a hand trolley from 1972.

Among the artifacts of great historical value is the gate of Queen Victoria, manufactured in Britain in 1902, and a warning gate from 1897. The museum also includes photographs showing the expansion of railway lines and the old council table of Atbara Municipality from 1951.

One of the most recent and rare acquisitions is the locomotive used during the British campaign in Sudan in 1897, during Kitchener Pasha's campaign.

  • Location: Al-Sudanah district, Atbara
  • Established: 2000
  • Founder: Sudanese Railways Corporation
  • Notable Artifacts:
    • Steam and diesel locomotives (1901)
    • Queen Victoria Gate (1902)
    • Kitchener campaign locomotive (1897)
    • Railway carriages (1901, 1904, 1912)
  • Historical Significance: Preserves Sudan's railway heritage
