Historical Timeline

Explore Sudan's rich history through significant eras and events that shaped its cultural heritage

8000 BCE

Prehistory

Neolithic communities settled along the Nile.

Development of agriculture, livestock herding,fortified villages, and early cemeteries (e.g., R12).

Cultural and genetic interaction with Upper Egypt.

Social complexity laid foundations for the Kingdom of Kush.

1700 BCE – 350 CE

Kingdom of Kush

Emergence of Kush with capitals at Kerma, Napata, and later Meroë. .

Kushite rule over Egypt as the 25th Dynasty (8th century BCE)

Construction of Nubian pyramids (El-Kurru, Nuri,Jebel Barkal, Meroë).

Conflicts with Assyrians and loss of Egypt

Decline and fall of Kush around 350 CE

350 – 1500

Christian Nubian Kingdoms

Formation of three kingdoms: Nobatia, Makuria,and Alodia.

Successful resistance to Arab invasions; signing of the Baqt treaty.

Afro-Byzantine culture, written Old Nubian language, high status of women.

Collapse by the late 15th century.

1500–1821

Islamic Sultanates of Sennar and Darfur

Establishment of the Funj Sultanate (Sennar) and Darfur Sultanate.

Spread of Islam and Arabization.

Periods of expansion followed by internal decline.

Political weakening before Ottoman-Egyptian conquest.

1821 – 1899

Turco–Egyptian Rule and Mahdist State

Heavy taxation and economic hardship.

Mahdist Revolution (1881–1898).

Capture of Khartoum and establishment of Mahdist state.

Defeat of Mahdists by British forces at Omdurman.

1899–1956

Anglo–Egyptian Condominium

Sudan governed jointly by Britain and Egypt,effectively under British control.

Administrative division between North and South.

Sudanese participation in World War II.

Rise of nationalist movements.

Independence of Sudan in 1956.
